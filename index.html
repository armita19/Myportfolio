More About Me

After graduating highschool during Covid, I studied a year at IADE universidade Europeia Lisboa in a Global Design Bachelor's degree which I thoroughly enjoyed, I transferred to Canada a year later due to Portugal not accepting A level cambridge high school diplomas anymore (which affected many international students). Regardless, after a year of getting on the dean's list in Canada and excelling at my multimedia design and development course, I had to return to Portugal due to citizenship requirements (although it was unfortunate I've realised I'm much happier in Europe!). Now, I have two years worth of Post-secondary education and even though things beyond my control keep trying to get in my way, my passion for Design and Technology has never faltered once!
Aside from my insurmountabe desire to finish my degree in design,
I also have many hobbies! I love to play different instruments as an ex-band kid (clarinet, piano, guitar, and the ukelele), I love to crochet, I love horse-riding, reading, painting, honestly all the whimsy stuff that makes life worth living!

Art and Creativity

My love for art began at a very young age.
Growing up, my mum was always painting something, constantly changing the look of the house, always obsessed with fashion, while my dad taught me the value of classical music and fine arts by taking me to many galleries and museums.
This combination shaped me as a I grew older, now, in Lisbon, my favourite place in the whole city is the Gulbenkian Museum, I regurlarly attend their exhibitions, symphonies, some events, and sometimes just go there to study.
In my free time, Im always looking for new creative things to try whether its 3d printing, embroidery, cool workshops and I hope this all will one day lead me to being a creative director at my own multimedia Design Company.
